PROJECT STORY

Robots that feel a little bit alive

GBLN-01 is not a product. It’s a sandbox for ideas: actuation, control, manufacturing, and the question of what a “general human-capable” robot might actually look like when you build it on a garage budget instead of a factory budget.

The goal is simple: a bipedal platform that can move around real human spaces, do light-duty, coarse manipulation, and be open enough that other people can learn from its failures and steal the good ideas.

TECH HIGHLIGHTS

What’s interesting here

Actuation

Compact, high-torque electric actuators built around frameless BLDC motors and printed gear stages. Everything is biased toward “good enough to walk and fall” rather than perfect machining.

Locomotion

Locomotion is driven by reinforcement learning controllers tested on subscale hardware first, then scaled to GBLN-01. Think: “teach a robot to walk, then see what else it can do.”

Manufacturing

Most structural parts come off consumer-grade printers. Metal is reserved for where stress actually demands it. The experiment is: how far can you go without a factory?

STATUS

Where things are right now

Actuator prototypes

First-generation actuators have been prototyped and tested on a dedicated rig to validate torque, thermal behavior, and backlash.

Subscale RL walker

Reinforcement-learning locomotion has been proven on a smaller test robot. GBLN-01 is the next platform for those controllers.

Full-body integration

Current focus is integrating the new actuators into a full-body frame, wiring up the control stack, and capturing enough footage to tell the story properly.

This site will evolve as the robot does. Expect broken parts, weird gaits, and occasional moments of “wait, that actually works.”
